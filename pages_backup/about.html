Our Story

How we started and where we are going

LANDLORDZS was founded in 2022 by a group of Cameroonian entrepreneurs and real-estate professionals who were tired of seeing property transactions fail because of opacity, fragmentation, and a lack of trusted intermediaries. What began as a simple listings board quickly evolved into a full-service platform connecting buyers, renters, sellers, agents, contractors, architects, lawyers, and vendors under one roof.

Headquartered in Douala — Cameroon's economic capital — with a second office in Yaoundé, the political and administrative heart of the country, LANDLORDZS is uniquely positioned to serve both the commercial powerhouse of the Littoral region and the rapidly growing institutional real-estate market of the Centre region. Our teams travel regularly to Buea, Bamenda, Limbe, and Kribi to ensure every corner of the country is represented on our platform.

Our mission is straightforward: to digitise Cameroon's real estate and construction market so that every citizen — whether they are searching for their first studio apartment in Douala's Akwa district or commissioning a commercial complex in Yaoundé's Bastos neighbourhood — can do so with confidence, speed, and full legal protection. We combine technology with local expertise to bring international-grade real-estate infrastructure to Central Africa.

Our Values

The principles that guide everything we do

🛡️

Trust & Transparency

Every listing, agent, contractor, and lawyer on our platform is verified. We publish reviews, credentials, and transaction histories so you can make decisions backed by facts, not promises.

💡

Innovation

We invest continuously in technology — from AI-powered property valuations to digital escrow and e-signing — so that Cameroonians have access to tools previously reserved for global real-estate markets.

🤝

Community

Real estate shapes neighbourhoods and lives. We partner with local governments, diaspora associations, and community groups across Yaoundé, Douala, Buea, Bamenda, Limbe, and Kribi to ensure growth is inclusive.
